My name is Griffin Leclerc, and since you are here, you probably wish to know more about me.

I am currently a Graduate Research Assistant at the University of New Hampshire Interoperability Laboratory.
My main leadership and development responsibilities are within the Avnu and IEEE 1588 working groups.
Avnu primarily focuses on Time Sensitive Networking technologies in the professional audio, automotive, and industrial spaces.
IEEE 1588 focuses on the Precision Time Protocol and its various profiles. PTP serves to synchronize time throughout a network.
My work entails writing and reviewing test plans derived from networking standards.
I also develop improvements for the IOL's time sensitive networking testing tool, Violett.
More information about my experience at the IOL is available on my resume.

I attend the University of New Hampshire, pursuing a Masters's degree in Computer Science. Expected December 2022.
I have previously obtained a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science as of May 2021.
