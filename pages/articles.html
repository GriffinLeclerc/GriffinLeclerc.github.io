From Tech Camp to the IOL (2016)

My first experience at the UNH IOL was through UNH's Tech Camp. Tech Camp is a two-week
summer program where middle and high school students are exposed to research being conducted
throughout the University.

During the first week of the program, students spend half-day periods covering 14 University
facilities in order to learn about the research conducted at each.
Prior to the second week, each student selects their preferred facility and spends
the remainder of their tech camp experience working in that field.

Intrigued, I chose the UNH IOL, for both years I attended tech camp. At the conclusion,
the IOL's program director suggested I apply for the internship program., which led to
being selected as an HighTech Bound Intern, seven years if student employment at the IOL,
a bachelors degree in Computer Science from UNH, and becoming a UNH Graduate Research Assistant.

This interview details my journey through the process.

The article summarizing the interview is available here.
A copy of the article can be viewed here.
